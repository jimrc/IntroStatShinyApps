Bootstrap Resampling

Start with a known "population". In this case our values are textbook costs for one semester at MSU in 10's of dollars.
  • We start with confidence level 95% and sample size n = 8. Change them later if you wish.
  • Click "Sample" and watch values get pulled from the population to the sample below.
    The population then disappears, because in real data collection we never know it.
    Bootstrap resamples are based on just the one sample.
  • Click "1 Resample". You'll see a re-sample of size 8 get selected from the original sample with replacement. Some of the items in the sample get chosen multiple times (giving a darker background), and some not at all (background stays white). The mean of the resample is shown as a point in the plot. Click "slower" or "faster" to vary the speed.
  • We'll need many samples, so click one of the "Many Resamples" options. Only the resample means are shown. A confidence interval appears on the plot as red boundary lines. It's computed by the "percentile" method.
